Regulatory risk examples

The biggest risk is not whether the AI can work. It is whether regulators, tribunals and professional bodies allow software to industrialise a process that has historically been slow, human and professionally gated.

Risk What could happen Example trigger Mitigation Severity
VOA tightens agent rules The VOA may require named human reviewers, professional certification, manual evidence checks, or tighter agent registration rules. Large volumes of AI-assisted Check or Challenge submissions start entering the system. Position as AI-assisted, professionally supervised. Use named accountable reviewers for higher-risk cases. High
Mass speculative appeal backlash Government or VOA could view automated filings as clogging the system and introduce higher thresholds, fees, or penalties. Thousands of weak cases are submitted because the software makes filing too easy. Only submit cases passing a conservative evidence threshold. Keep a full audit trail and quality score. High
Professional advice boundary Authorities may argue that valuation advice requires qualified surveyor oversight rather than unsupervised AI recommendations. The product markets itself as “replacing surveyors” or giving definitive valuation advice. Use MRICS oversight, careful language, and a formal review process before submission. High
Evidence admissibility challenge Tribunals may challenge AI-generated measurements, comparables or valuation reasoning unless explainable and independently defensible. A contested case reaches tribunal and the AI logic cannot be explained clearly. Generate human-readable evidence packs, comparable rationale, source data logs and measurement records. Medium
Liability for inaccurate submissions Incorrect scans, misclassified space, or overconfident savings estimates could create claims, complaints or reputational damage. The AI submits inaccurate floor data or makes a misleading saving estimate. Use disclaimers, validation gates, professional review, insurance and conservative confidence scoring. High
Consumer protection scrutiny No-win, no-fee models can attract scrutiny if customers misunderstand fees, timelines, appeal odds or actual savings. Marketing claims imply guaranteed savings or hide appeal duration and fee mechanics. Clear fee examples, transparent timelines, cooling-off terms and plain-English customer consent screens. Medium
GDPR and spatial data risk Interior scans may expose sensitive commercial layouts, security arrangements, stock areas or operational details. A data breach or unclear use of scans for model training. Data minimisation, encryption, retention limits, client consent, and clear separation between case data and model training data. Medium

VOA changes the Business Rates Valuation Account interface or restricts automated access. Keep manual fallback operations, monitoring, change detection and avoid relying only on headless browser automation. Medium

Recommended positioning

Do not position RateCheck AI as “AI replacing surveyors.” That invites regulatory resistance. Position it as an AI-assisted rating optimisation platform supervised by qualified professionals. The message should be: better access, better evidence, better auditability — not mass automated appeals.

Safer MVP path

Start with the lowest-risk, highest-trust workflow. Prove the value before attempting full autonomy.

Scan

Let a business scan its premises and generate a structured floor-area record.

Compare

Compare measured data with VOA records and local comparable properties.

Review

Route viable cases through human or MRICS-supervised review before submission.

File

Assist with Check / Challenge submission, then automate more as confidence grows.

Founder reality check

The idea is strong — but the plan needs the harder version

The business case is compelling because the market pain is real, the timing is sharp, and the ROI is measurable. But the plan should absorb the risk logic from this explainer: RateCheck AI should be framed as a supervised, evidence-led optimisation platform — not a fully autonomous filing engine.

What is genuinely strong

  • Real market pain: business rates are expensive, poorly understood and emotionally charged.
  • Strong timing: the 2026 revaluation creates a natural urgency window.
  • Sharp SME wedge: smaller occupiers are underserved by traditional rating advisors.
  • Clear ROI: customers can understand the value in pounds saved, not abstract productivity gains.
  • Contingency fit: no-win, no-fee aligns with how SMEs are likely to buy.

What is currently oversold

  • Autonomous agent framing: this is the biggest commercial and regulatory trigger.
  • RICS review threshold: reviewing only cases above £50k RV leaves most SME cases unsupervised.
  • Success-rate assumptions: 65% success and 30–40% viability should be labelled as Phase 1 hypotheses.
  • Revenue timing: Challenge cases may push revenue 12–18 months out, delaying breakeven.
  • Insurance costs: legal, compliance and PI cover are probably materially understated.

Specific investor fixes

  • Replace “AI replacing surveyors” language with “AI-assisted, professionally supervised”.
  • Change the RICS review model from a high-value threshold to a risk-based review threshold.
  • Add CAC and partner revenue-share assumptions to the unit economics.
  • Correct “OAuth delegation” to the actual VOA agent-code authorisation flow.
  • Split Year 1 revenue into Check-stage revenue and delayed Challenge-stage pipeline.

Better operating thesis

  • Phase 1: scan, compare, score and professionally review.
  • Phase 2: assisted filing with structured evidence packs.
  • Phase 3: automate low-risk, repeatable workflow steps.
  • Phase 4: expand into broader commercial property tax optimisation.
  • Moat: proprietary scan data, appeal outcomes and operational process intelligence.
Phase 1 revenue model Check-stage only

Separate near-term revenue from delayed Challenge-stage pipeline so Year 1 does not depend on 12–18 month outcomes.

PI and compliance cover 2–3x uplift

Model legal, compliance and professional indemnity as a major operating cost, not a light SaaS overhead.

Professional review 100%

Risk-review all Phase 1 cases before filing, not just properties above a £50k rateable value threshold.

Honest summary

The business plan reads like a fundraising document. This explainer should act as the founder's discipline layer. The idea is good enough to deserve a tougher plan: one that treats regulatory trust, PI exposure, evidence quality, CAC, and revenue lag as core design constraints rather than footnotes.
